How do I get the best range and energy efficiency out of my FEV Scooters
Here are a few tips to get the best performance out of your FEV Scooters
  • Do not hold brakes while riding. Though it is a common practice among scooter riders to hold the rear brakes ever so slightly while riding, it should be avoided as it reduces the life of your brakes and consumes extra energy in the process, thus reducing your efficiency.
  • Rapid acceleration and riding at high speeds also uses up your battery faster.
  • Maintain the recommended tyre pressure and check periodically (refer to the owner's manual for correct tyre pressures for your vehicle). If they are under-inflated, their rolling resistance increases as a result, more energy is consumed to move your scooter.
The motor used is an energy efficient low noise BLDC hub motor with 2.5 kW peak power and has been put through rigorous tests in the harshest weather and road conditions.

Charging

The charging times for different variants are as under:
  • FEV KORO: It takes approximately 4 hours 30 mins to charge from 0 - 80% with the standard 650 W charger.
  • FEV NIXDLX: It takes approximately 4 hours 30 mins to charge from 0 - 80% with the standard 650 W charger.
  • FEV POWER+: It takes approximately 4 hours 30 mins to charge from 0 - 80% with the standard 650 W charger
It takes around 3.5 kWh of electricity to fully charge the FEV Scooters. This is equivalent to 3.5 units of electricity. Assuming the cost of electricity to be 7, it would cost you just 24.50 for charging your FEV Scooters from 0% to 100%. This will allow you to cover a distance of 100km per charge.
Fast charging impacts the lifespan of Li-ion batteries and as of now FEV Scooters does not support fast charging. We are continuously working on fast charging technology that has the least impact on battery life and would bring it to market at the earliest.
The FEV Scooters comes with a 650 W plug-and-play portable charger that can be used to charge a vehicle at any standard 15 A domestic socket.
Your FEV Scooters comes with a standard 650 W charger that can plug into a 15 A household socket and does not require a separate line or meter to function. In case your designated parking spot is far away from your residence, you may need to arrange for a separate connection.
The FEV Scooters charging socket only consumes nominal energy - less than a night lamp - in standby mode.
The charger itself has a cooling fan built in, which cuts in and out depending on the operating temperature. Apart from the fan noise from the new 650 W off-board charger for the FEV Scooters variants, the charger emits no other noise.
